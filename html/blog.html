Traveling

July 17, 2017

Hey everyone! This is the first blog post of hopefully many. I just wanted to give a brief introduction about myself and what it is I do.

In June I decided to start focusing on traveling a bit more seriously. The company I work for allows for some of it's employees to work remotely. While I'm not full time remote I have gone on several week long trips around the country and hopefully soon around the world!

Last week I was up in Alaska staying at a fun little hostel, eating some great seafood and enjoying the beautiful sights of Anchorage and the nature around it. The few days before that I was in Seattle where I used to live just getting a few days of exploration in.

My next big trip is Barcelona at the beginning of September. I recently moved over to NYC which kind of throws in a wrench in those plans, but I'm still hoping to make it work. It will be my first visit to Europe and first visit out of the country as an adult.

I'd like to talk about the challenges of working/traveling. The first is expenses of course. Staying in hostels and going out to eat will burn through your budget very fast. A hostel at 30 to 35 a night is still around 1000 a month. Plus if you don't want to work at a hostel you normally have to work at a cafe which is another 3 to 5 dollars a day on coffee (assuming you only have 1).

One of the greatest challenges I faced though was being too tempted by the surroundings to actually put a full day of work in. I found myself having to get out of the hostel and into a quiet cafe or bar to actually not be distracted too much. I know when I get to Barcelona this is going to be a massive challenge. When the weekends roll around though it's amazing. Being able to explore Anchorage was awesome and I definitely want to go back and check it out.

The next biggest challenge of working remotely is finding a decent remote contract. This is what stops most travelers from traveling around the world while working. Unless you want to live in a cheap country and survive on barely anything then you definitely need to land something pretty decent. I got very lucky with my past few clients and am now setup in a good place to land almost anything I need.

Overall I would say that working and traveling is definitely an amazing experience if you can manage your time and budget correctly. My past few trips have been such a learning experience of just letting go of a lot of possessions and embracing new cultures.
